What is the side effect from sevoflurane?
Sevoflurane (Volatile Liquid For Inhalation) is an anesthetic drug used to induce and maintain general anesthesia in adult and adult patients during surgery. Ultane is available in generic form. The common side effects of Ultane include drowsiness and dizziness. If you experience it, tell your doctor if you have anxiety, chills, respiratory problems, changes in urine, seizures, muscle stiffness, tight or irregular heartbeat, stomach pain, tiredness, eyes or yellowing of the skin, or dark urine. Side Effects Drug Center provides a comprehensive picture of available drug information about potential side effects when taking this drug.

Is sevoflurane safe for pregnant and lactating women?
There is no adequate research on the risks of using this drug in pregnant or lactating women. Always consult your doctor to consider potential benefits and risks before using this medication. These drugs fall into the risk of pregnancy category B according to the US Food and Drug Administration (FDA).

What drugs might interact with sevoflurane?
Some drugs that might interact with Sevoflurane. Tell your doctor if you are taking any other medications, especially one of the following: Amiodarone, droxidopa, or labetalol because side effects, such as an increased risk of low or high blood pressure and other heart complications, may occur.
